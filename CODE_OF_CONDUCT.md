# Project Conduct

Long Arc Protocol exists to make careful technical collaboration possible.
Participation requires respect for people, evidence, boundaries, and the
reversibility of shared work.

## Expected conduct

- Discuss ideas and implementations without demeaning people.
- State uncertainty, provenance, and limits plainly.
- Keep review focused on the contribution and its documented effects.
- Respect privacy, confidentiality, attribution, and consent.
- Accept correction without retaliation.
- Help maintain an environment where people can participate without
  harassment or intimidation.

## Unacceptable conduct

Harassment, threats, discriminatory abuse, deliberate humiliation, sexualized
attention, doxxing, impersonation, credential exposure, and knowingly false
provenance or conformance claims are not acceptable.

## Scope and response

This policy initially applies only to this repository, its pull requests, and
the GitHub communication surfaces explicitly opened for the project. It does
not open off-platform events, direct community channels, or private
implementation surfaces.

For disruptive repository content, use GitHub's **Report content** control when
it is available. For platform-wide abuse or urgent safety concerns, use
GitHub's own reporting and escalation paths. Do not publish sensitive personal
information in an issue, pull request, discussion, or report.

Broader off-platform or direct community spaces will not open until the project
owner provides a private, project-controlled reporting route. Project
maintainers may remove content, restrict participation, or refer serious safety
concerns to the appropriate platform or authority. Responses should be
proportionate, documented, privacy-preserving, and open to correction when
facts change.

Reports should include only the information needed to understand and address
the concern.

No maintainer, contributor, tool, or automated process is exempt from this
policy.
